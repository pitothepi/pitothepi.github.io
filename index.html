CFD in Solidworks FlowSim

[hide]

My senior project involved the creation of an unfolding UAV. Among other tasks, I performed detailed simulation of our plane in Solidworks' Computational Fluid Dynamics suite. The simulation was quite thorough, including a rotating region for the propeller and multiple runs with a number of initial conditions to obtain information about the stability and aerodynamic performance of the aircraft.

This is one image from the results of the simulation. I chose it because it demonstrates many of the insights gained:

  • There is a large stagnation zone behind the fuselage that contributes to the design's low L/D.
  • In addition to the expected wingtip vortices, there are additional vortices behind the wing joints.
  • The flow acceleration from the prop is rather significant, which will affect lift generated by the wing.

This image shows the angle of attack sweep, and the effect of the propwash on the wing. It was used to decide what the maximum angle of attack was, which I compared to results from wing theory.

This is an example of some of the calculations that were done with the numerical results from the simulation. From these results, I found the ideal trim conditions for the aircraft for both maximum range and endurance, as well as a host of other details.
